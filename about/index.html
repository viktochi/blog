The Scoop!

Born and raised in Nigeria — born in the South and raised in the Central-North. In the span of over two decades, I have learnt valuable lessons from my interactions with people from around the world. And in all of that murkiness of figuring out life, I have found that nothing beats a good sleep after a day's work.

After I was born, life happened bla bla bla, I graduated from UC Berkeley after 4 years, served my country for a year, and now looking to achieve my childhood dreams of working around the clock while creating memorable moments that I will share with my kids.

I am not an overthinker, but sometimes I try to find meaning in things that seem stupid just the way I am trying to find meaning in my life right now. At the moment, I am probably thinking of my next blog post, working or sleeping.

The greatest advice I have ever gotten is to wear my mind like a garment and wash it whenever it was dirty — I gave myself this advice.

To answer the big question above — there is a lot to know about people, but there isn't much to know about me, although I like to believe I know a lot about myself.

|—| At 16, I wrote this letter to my friends |—|

The world is indeed chaotic and full of problems as well as beautiful things and people all encompassed in nature so unimaginably designed.

Our duty however, is to become resolute in our trajectory to make the world a better place one step at a time through smiles and actions as the years go by — that is my aim in life and I hope it is yours too.

Therefore, here lies both the freedoms and excesses of this world, and here is your heart. Feel it, touch it, own it, and don't let it fail.

I fear each day for what might be, but I am extremely excited for the things that I will wrath.
So walk with me.

By Viktor Tochi
